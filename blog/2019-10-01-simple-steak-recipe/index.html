Simple Steak Recipe

October 1, 2019

By: Jennifer Vespes

The trick to making steak lies in two things: the source and the cooking. Grass-fed organic beef is a great source for quality steak that can take a meal from good to melt-in-your-mouth amazing! Our chef’s go-to cut is grass-fed filet mignon because of its buttery texture. This cut comes from the tenderloin and if you love steak this is the cut to get! Here is a simple way to prepare it for a quick dinner.

Simple Steak (feeds 2 people)

Ingredients

  • 2 – 3oz filet mignon steaks
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons crushed garlic
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 sprigs of rosemary
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar

Instructions

  1. 1 Set the steaks out on the counter for 10-15 minutes before you cook to bring them to room temperature.
  2. 2 Mix olive oil, garlic, salt, pepper, and balsamic vinegar in a small bowl. Brush the mixture over the steaks and set aside.
  3. 3 Heat in oven safe skillet to medium. While you’re waiting for it to come to temperature turn the oven on to 450 degrees.
  4. 4 Brown all sides of the steak to seal the outside, which will limit moisture loss during cooking.
  5. 5 Pour a little of the mixture over the steaks in the pan and add the rosemary. Put the pan in the oven.
  6. 6 Cook the steaks in the oven to your preferred doneness. Our chef recommends a medium steak of 10-15 minutes in the oven.
  7. 7 Pull out and let the steak rest in the pan for 10 minutes. This is important so the moisture is reabsorbed into the cells instead of pouring out onto the plate when you cut it.
  8. 8 Eat with a salad or your favorite vegetable.
  9. 9 Enjoy!
