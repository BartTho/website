Spotlight: Santa Rosa, California

October 15, 2019

By: Brett Dolnichio

At Two Trees Olive Oil, our headquarters is more than just the location of our building. We’re located in Santa Rosa, California, a community we call home. With a growing population of nearly 180,000 residents, Santa Rosa has become the central hub of Northern California for both residents and tourists alike.

Nestled in Sonoma County, 55 miles north of San Francisco, the area is well-known for their vineyards, but olives grow just as plentifully in the northern California region. Visitors to Santa Rosa can expect both great indoor and outdoor adventures, including nearby hiking, cycling, backpacking, and camping locations, as well as shopping districts, restaurants and dining, art galleries, and plenty of concerts and cultural events.

If you’re looking for something more adventurous, check in at the Sonoma County Airport where helicopter rides are available daily. For those looking for the opportunity to slow down and relax, many of the hotels in the area are well-equipped with restaurants and day spas. It’s a great way to pamper yourself without even leaving your hotel.

Nearby Trione-Annadel State Park has more than 40 miles of hiking, biking, and horseback riding trails, picnic areas, access to Lake Ilsanjo, and guided tours. Trione-Annadel is home to hundreds of varieties of wildflowers, with the majority of blooms occurring in May through June, although some varieties bloom year-round.

We offer daily tours, olive oil tastings, and a summer concert series with local artists performing in our outdoor picnic area every weekend, weather permitting. For more information on our upcoming events, visit www.twotreesoliveoil.com/tours-events/events-private-parties.

Brett Dolnichio is Two Tree’s Marketing Manager. Brett’s been with the company for many years and has a deep understanding of the Two Tree’s brand, products, and customers. He’s our biggest fan and loves the excitement of bringing new olive oil products to market. In his free time, he loves camping completely off the map.
