How We Make Our Olive Oil

Our extra-virgin olive oil (EVOO) is made with equal parts love and labor, and always with the utmost attention to quality, purity, and flavor.

Agriculture

Cultivating

1

Our olive trees are diligently pruned, watered, and monitored for pests while the fruit is growing. In addition, we apply organic farming techniques to ensure we can carry the “organic” label.

Harvesting

2

We harvest the greener, more unripe olives, to preserve quality and nutritional value. Harvesting is carried out by hand shaking the trees, or by using the newer mechanical harvesters, with the main goal of preventing damage to the fruit.

Milling

Cleaning

3

Within twelve hours of picking, harvested olives are transported to the mill where they are dropped into a hopper and move along a conveyor belt to be washed, sorted, and cleaned of branches and leaves.

Crushing

4

Inside the crusher, the olives are ground into a paste to facilitate the release of oil.

Malaxing

5

The resulting paste moves into the malaxer to mix the small oil droplets with the larger ones. We minimize the malaxing time as much as possible, to avoid heating up the oil and degrading its quality.

Extraction

Two-Phase Centrifuge

6

The paste moves into a two-phase centrifuge, where it’s spun through a series of cylindrical gates, causing the liquid to be separated from the paste.

Paste

The paste, which includes the olive skin and pit, is dried and used for animal feed.

Oil & Water

The liquid, consisting of oil and water, moves onto the vertical separator.

Vertical Separator

7

The oil and water mixture is spun again to separate the oil from the water.

Water

The extracted water, which contains valuable minerals, is recycled as fertilizer and used on our olive groves.

Extra Virgin Olive Oil

8

The oil leaves the separator free from water, with only minimal particles from the paste. The extracted olive oil is then filtered and stored in stainless steel containers at about 65 ℉, before it’s bottled and shipped out.
